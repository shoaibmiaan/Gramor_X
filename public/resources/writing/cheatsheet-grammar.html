IELTS Writing — Grammar Cheatsheet

Accuracy first. Clean > fancy. Use these as quick checks when drafting and editing.

1) Sentence Types

  • Simple: One clause — Cities are growing.
  • Compound: Two independent clauses — Costs rise, and residents move.
  • Complex: Main + sub — Although costs rise, many stay for jobs.

Tip: Aim for a mix; keep every sentence clear.

2) Common Clause Openers

  • Although (concession), because (reason), while (contrast), which (relative).
  • Use that for clarity in complex noun clauses: It is clear that …

3) Article & Countable Nouns

  • Singular countable → article: a policy, the policy.
  • Uncountable → no plural “s”: traffic, information, research.

4) Subject–Verb Agreement

  • Data (uncountable in IELTS writing): the data shows.
  • None of the solutions is perfect.

5) Modifier Placement

  • Place modifiers near what they modify: Policies that raise funding for schools are popular.
  • Avoid dangling: To reduce emissions, the city introduced a bus lane.

6) Punctuation Essentials

  • Comma + coordinator (and, but, so…) for compound sentences.
  • No comma between subject and verb; avoid run-ons.

7) Formal Tone

  • Avoid contractions: use do not, cannot.
  • Avoid slang and second person you (use people, individuals).

8) Quick Proofing Pass

  • One error-free complex sentence per paragraph.
  • Check articles, agreement, and comma splices.
